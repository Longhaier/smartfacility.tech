# HVAC Central Air-Conditioning Energy-Saving Transformation: Cool Down Your Electricity Bill

## Central Air-Conditioning = "Half the Territory" of Factory Electricity Costs?

In most factories, central air-conditioning system electricity expenses account for 30%-50% of the total electricity bill.

A certain electronics factory has 3 sets of 800-ton centrifugal chillers, each with a rated power of 560kW, running at full load during summer. However, actual measurements showed: chiller COP (coefficient of performance) was only 4.2, far below the design value of 5.5; chilled water temperature was set at 7℃, but actual demand was only 9℃; fan coil unit filters were severely clogged, causing insufficient air volume, forcing the units to "work overtime."

Over the year, this "unnecessary electricity bill" exceeded 1.2 million yuan.

## Three Major Causes of High Central Air-Conditioning Energy Consumption

### 1. Low Host Efficiency

**COP (Coefficient of Performance) Definition:**
> COP = Cooling Capacity(kW) ÷ Input Power(kW)

**Industry Standards:**
- Level 1 Energy Efficiency: COP ≥ 5.5
- Level 2 Energy Efficiency: COP ≥ 5.0
- Level 3 Energy Efficiency: COP ≥ 4.5

Below 4.5? Your chiller needs a health check.

### 2. Improper Chilled Water Temperature Settings

**Common Problem:** Temperature set too low

**Optimization Space:**
- Every 1℃ increase in temperature reduces host energy consumption by 3-5%
- Within process allowable range, try to increase chilled water temperature

**Recommended Settings:**
- Comfort air conditioning: 7-10℃
- Process cooling: 9-12℃

### 3. Terminal Equipment Clogging

**Consequences of Filter Clogging:**
- Air volume decreased by 20-30%
- Energy consumption increased by 15-25%
- Cooling effect deteriorated

## 5 Practical Energy-Saving Solutions

### 1. Chiller Group Control Optimization

**Core Strategy:**
- Automatically adjust number of operating units based on load
- Avoid "large horse pulling small cart"
- Implement chiller rotation to extend service life

**Energy-Saving Effect:** 15-25%

### 2. Chilled Water Temperature Optimization

**Specific Actions:**
- Evaluate process requirements, reasonably increase set temperature
- Time-of-day adjustment: Can appropriately increase at night
- Connect to BAS system for intelligent adjustment

**Case:** A factory increased chilled water temperature from 7℃ to 9℃, saving 180,000 yuan annually in electricity costs.

### 3. Regular Fan Coil Maintenance

**Maintenance Checklist:**
| Item | Cycle | Key Points |
|------|-------|------------|
| Filter cleaning | Monthly | Prevent clogging |
| Fan cleaning | Quarterly | Reduce resistance |
| Coil cleaning | Half year | Improve heat exchange efficiency |
| Belt replacement | Annually | Ensure transmission efficiency |

### 4. Cooling Tower and Condenser Optimization

**Cooling Tower Cleaning:**
- Clean monthly
- Check if water distributor is even
- Filler intact, no damage

**Condenser Cleaning:**
- Physical cleaning half yearly
- Chemical cleaning annually
- Keep heat exchange tube inner walls clean

**Effect:** Chiller COP can be improved by 0.3-0.8

### 5. Fresh Air and Interior Zone Optimization

**Zone Control:**
- Office area: 8:00-18:00 operation
- Production area: Control according to production shifts
- Warehouse: Adjust according to temperature requirements

**Night Strategy:**
- Non-production areas turn off after work
- On-duty areas run at lower temperature
- Avoid 24-hour full operation
