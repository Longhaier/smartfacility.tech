# Transformer Load Optimization: Making Power Systems More Efficient

Electricity is the blood of modern industry, and transformers are the "heart" of power systems. How transformers are selected and operated in factory power supply systems directly relates to power efficiency and safety.

## Understanding Transformer Losses

Transformers have two types of losses during operation:

### 1. No-Load Loss (Core Loss)
- Cause: Core magnetization
- Characteristics: Exists as long as power is connected, regardless of load

### 2. Load Loss (Copper Loss)
- Cause: Winding resistance heating
- Characteristics: Proportional to the square of load rate

## What is the Optimal Load Rate?

When no-load loss equals load loss, total loss is minimized:

```
Load Rate = √(No-load Loss / Load Loss)
```

Common transformer economic load rates:

- S11 type: 50-60%
- S13 type: 45-55%
- Amorphous alloy: 40-50%
